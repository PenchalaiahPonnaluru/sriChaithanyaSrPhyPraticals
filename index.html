Physics Lab Notebook

Instructions

Follow these steps to prepare your physics lab record book:

  1. Step 1: Prepare the record book
    • Cover: Create a cover page containing your name, class, roll number, school name, and the academic year.
    • Index: Leave the first page empty for the index. Fill in the experiment number, title, and page number as you complete each practical.
    • Materials: Use a black or blue pen for all writing on the ruled pages. Use a pencil, scale, and compass for diagrams and tables on the plain pages.
  2. Step 2: Write on the ruled (right-side) page
    • Aim: State the objective of the experiment concisely.
    • Apparatus: List all the materials and equipment used in the experiment.
    • Theory/Principle: Briefly explain the underlying scientific principle or law. Include any relevant formulas.
    • Procedure: Describe the experiment's steps in detail, following the sequence of execution.
    • Result: State the final value of the quantity measured, including the correct units.
    • Precautions: List at least three steps taken to ensure accuracy and safety.
    • Sources of Error: List at least two possible factors that could have affected your results.
  3. Step 3: Write on the plain (left-side) page
    • Diagram/Circuit Diagram: Draw a neat, labeled diagram of the experimental setup or circuit.
    • Observation Table: Draw a neat table to record raw data and readings. Enter values using a pen.
    • Calculations: Show detailed steps of calculations, including formulas and final results with proper units.
    • Graph: If required, plot the graph on graph paper. Label axes with appropriate quantities and units.
  4. Step 4: Finalize the experiment entry
    • Date and Signature: Write the date of the experiment in the margin. Obtain the teacher's signature if required.
    • Proofread: Review your entry for neatness, clarity, and errors. Avoid using white-out; cross out mistakes with a single line.
